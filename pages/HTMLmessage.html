HTML Messages

In HTML, messages are usually displayed using simple elements like <div> or <p> with different styles to show alerts, success, warnings, etc. Although HTML doesn’t have a special tag for messages, we can style messages using CSS.

Types of Messages

  • Success: Indicates a successful action (like form submitted).
  • Warning: Shows a caution or attention needed.
  • Error: Tells about a failure or mistake.
  • Info: Just a useful note or information.

Example Code

<div style="color: green; border: 1px solid green; padding: 10px;">
  Success! Your form has been submitted.
</div>

<div style="color: red; border: 1px solid red; padding: 10px;">
  Error! Something went wrong.
</div>

<div style="color: orange; border: 1px solid orange; padding: 10px;">
  Warning! Please check your input.
</div>

<div style="color: blue; border: 1px solid blue; padding: 10px;">
  Info: You can update your profile anytime.
</div>

Output

✅ Success! Your form has been submitted.
❌ Error! Something went wrong.
⚠️ Warning! Please check your input.
ℹ️ Info: You can update your profile anytime.
Summary:

HTML messages are created using normal tags like <div> or <p>, and styled with CSS to look like success, warning, error, or info messages. They help users understand what is happening on a web page.
